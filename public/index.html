Objectives

PsychonautWiki is a community-driven encyclopedia which is striving to formally catalog the field of psychonautics in a legitimate and comprehensive manner. Our primary motivations include:

  • documenting all aspects of psychonautics from a sceptical, reason-based, and scientific perspective and separating the field from its associated mysticism
  • minimizing danger, encouraging safe practices, and rectifying cultural taboos associated with personal use of psychoactive substances by providing accurate information without bias
  • utilizing open source software to facilitate content creation and the progressive goal-driven completion of this project
  • serving as a community-driven platform through which individuals of similar interests can discuss, collaborate, network, and organize

753 articles are available

Participate

Contributions are appreciated and users are encouraged to collaborate with the community. Our primary methods of collaboration include:

  • engaging in constructive discussion with staff and community members through our IRC channel and by emailing us directly
  • creating new articles and helping with tasks described on our to-do list in accordance with the community guidelines
  • improving upon existing content by removing errors, grammar checking, and expanding existing articles
  • kindly helping us fund and maintain our expensive server via bitcoin, paypal or patreon donations
